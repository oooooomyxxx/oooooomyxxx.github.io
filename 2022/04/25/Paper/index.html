Three pass approch

The first pass

To get a bird's eye view of the paper(5-10 minutes)

  1. Carefully read the title, abstract, and introduction
  2. Read the section and sub-section heading, but ignore everything else
  3. Read the conclusions
  4. Glance over the references, mentally ticking off the ones you’ve already read

At the end of the first pass, you should be able to answer the five Cs:

  1. Category: What type of paper is this?
  2. Context: Which other papers is it related to? Which theoretical bases were used to analyze the problem?
  3. Correctness: Do the assumptions appear to be valid?
  4. Contributions: What are the paper’s main contributes?
  5. Clarity: Is the paper well written?

Then, choose whether to read further.

The second pass

Read the paper with greater care, but ignore details such as proofs.It helps to jot down the key points, or to make comments in the margins, as you read.(Take up to 1h.)

  1. Look carefully at the figures, diagrams and other illustration.
  2. Remember to mark relevant unread references for further reading.

After this pass, you should be able to grasp the content of the paper.

The third pass

Attempt to virtually re-implement the paper: making the same assumptions as the author, re-create the work.

By comparing the re-creation with the actual paper, you can easily identify not only a paper’s innovations, but also its hidden failings and assumptions.(4-5h→1h)

At the end of this pass, you should be able to reconstruct the entire structure of the paper from memory, as well as be able to identity its strong and weak points.
